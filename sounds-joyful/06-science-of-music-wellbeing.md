## What Music Does to the Brain

### Dopamine and Oxytocin
When we make music together — especially through rhythm and voice — our brains release dopamine (reward, pleasure) and oxytocin (bonding, trust). These chemicals create feelings of connection and wellbeing within minutes.

### Neurological Synchronisation
Research shows that when people drum or sing together, their brainwaves and heart rates can synchronise. We literally align with each other. This creates a sense of cohesion that is hard to achieve through discussion alone.

### Stress Reduction
Music activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the "rest and digest" mode. Cortisol drops. Heart rate variability improves. People feel calmer and more grounded.

## Why Group Music Works for Teams

**Shared experience creates belonging.**

The strongest predictor of wellbeing at work is a sense of belonging. Music creates shared experience faster than almost any other activity. Everyone participates. Everyone contributes. No one is left out.

**It bypasses the thinking brain.**

Unlike traditional team-building exercises, musical experiences don't require people to "perform" socially. There's no right answer. No competition. The brain relaxes and connection happens more naturally.

**It builds trust.**

When people create something together — a rhythm, a song, a moment of harmony — they experience collaboration in its purest form. That trust carries back into the workplace.
